Forest City: Malaysia's most ambitious Special Financial Zone.

An artificial island off the southern tip of Johor, redesignated as a Special Financial Zone in 2024 — changing the investment conversation entirely for buyers who know what to look for.

🏝 Special Financial Zone
What is Forest City?

An island township 30 minutes from Singapore, now with SFZ status.

Forest City is a 1,386-hectare man-made island development located in Gelang Patah, at the south-western tip of Johor — one of the closest points in Malaysia to Singapore. It sits roughly 30 minutes from the Second Link (Tuas) checkpoint by car.

Originally developed as a large-scale mixed-use township by Country Garden Pacificview, Forest City has undergone a significant policy repositioning since 2024 when the Malaysian government designated it a Special Financial Zone (SFZ) — bringing targeted tax incentives, a duty-free zone, and a single-family office framework that have shifted it from a stalled development story into an active investment zone.

For buyers, this matters because SFZ status changes both the investment rationale and the pool of future occupants — financial services firms, family offices, and multinational companies are being actively attracted to set up there, which underpins longer-term demand in a way that pure residential demand alone did not.

📍 Location: Gelang Patah, Iskandar Malaysia, Johor Bahru — on a man-made island in the Strait of Johor.
🚗 Distance: ~30 min to Second Link (Tuas) · ~45 min to Johor Bahru city centre · ~60 min to Singapore CBD via Second Link.
✈️ Nearest airport: Senai International Airport ~45 min · Singapore Changi Airport ~75 min via Second Link.
Key facts

What SFZ status actually means for buyers

💰
0% tax on certain income

Qualifying financial services companies and family offices operating within the SFZ can access preferential tax treatment — attracting high-net-worth occupants.

🏖
Duty-free zone

Forest City operates as a duty-free island for certain goods — adding a lifestyle dimension that differentiates it from mainland Johor developments.

🌏
Foreign ownership ease

Units in Forest City have lower minimum purchase thresholds compared to mainland landed property — making it one of the more accessible entry points for international buyers.

Market brief

What's happening in Forest City right now

The honest picture — what's changed since SFZ designation and what still needs to play out.

Sentiment has shifted positively since the SFZ announcement, with secondary market prices recovering from lows seen during the development's difficult 2020–2023 period. Buyers who dismissed Forest City a few years ago are looking again.

That said, occupancy and activiation are still building — the SFZ framework is real but its full effect depends on how quickly financial services firms and family offices actually set up operations there. It is a medium-to-long term play, not an immediate yield story.

The clearest near-term case is for buyers who want island lifestyle at a price point not available anywhere else this close to Singapore — resort-style facilities, golf, beachfront access, and a car-free internal environment, at a fraction of Singapore or even Sentosa costs.
