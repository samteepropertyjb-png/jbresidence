Iskandar Puteri — Township Guide

Sunway City Iskandar Puteri: The 2,000-Acre Township Explained

Sam Tee Property Advisor · REN 80322
July 2026

Sunway City Iskandar Puteri is one of the most ambitious integrated township projects in Johor — and one of the least well-understood. Most people know the name but have only a surface-level picture of what it actually is. This article gives you the factual overview: what the township is, how it is structured, what makes it different from other large developments in Iskandar Puteri, and what the implications are for buyers and investors.

The numbers

  • Total size: 2,000 acres
  • Gross Development Value: RM30 billion
  • Developer: Sunway Property (part of Sunway Group)
  • Location: Iskandar Puteri, Johor — western corridor, Second Link
  • Distance from Singapore: 5km via Second Link (Tuas) expressway
  • Current population: Approximately 125,000 residents, workers, and students
  • Security: 120 security officers including 30 auxiliary police, 88 CCTVs, 24/7 surveillance

The five pillars: Live, Work, Play, Learn, Stay

The township is built around five integrated functions — not just residential, which is how most property developments are conceived.

Live — Residential precincts including landed homes, high-rise condominiums, and serviced apartments at various price points within the masterplan.

Work — Commercial and office components including designer offices and smart office towers. The township positions itself as an emerging business hub for the Second Link corridor, drawing on proximity to Singapore's western industrial zones.

Play — Over 50 F&B outlets, 30 leisure and entertainment attractions, 20 wellness facilities including Abbashi Wellness, Sunway Hotel Big Box, XPark Chalet, Makan Wheel, and the Causeway Link CW7L bus terminal at Sunway Big Box.

Learn — Sunway International School (Canadian/IB, K–Grade 12), SJK(C) Cheah Fah (GreenRE Platinum), The Treehouse Playschool, Sunway TES accounting college, and multiple enrichment schools including a free coding school (42 Iskandar Puteri), equestrian school, swim school, and gymnastics school.

Stay — Sunway Hotel Big Box and XPark Chalet offer hospitality options within the township, which is relevant both for visitors to the commercial components and for corporate tenants needing short-term accommodation.

The Build, Own, Operate model — why it matters

Most property developers in Malaysia build residential units, sell them, collect the GDV, and move on. The ongoing quality of commercial facilities, parks, management, and infrastructure then depends on whoever takes over — often management corporations (JMBs) or commercial tenants who have no particular incentive to maintain the original vision.

Sunway Property operates differently. As a Build, Own, Operate developer, Sunway retains ownership of the commercial infrastructure, hotels, retail components, and key amenities within the township. This means Sunway has a direct and ongoing financial interest in the quality of the township experience — if the hotel underperforms, Sunway loses money. If the retail is poorly managed, Sunway feels it. This structural alignment of incentives between developer and township quality is rare in Malaysian property development and is the single most important differentiator of Sunway City as an investment.

Buyers in Sunway City are not betting on a developer who will complete the project and exit — they are buying into a township where the developer has an ongoing commercial stake in the environment remaining high quality.

Security infrastructure

The security setup at Sunway City is unusually comprehensive for a Malaysian township. 120 security officers — including 30 licensed auxiliary police — operate 24/7 across the development, supported by 88 CCTVs, mobile patrols, panic button systems, emergency response teams, and horse patrols within the park areas. For families with children who want to allow reasonable freedom of movement within the community, this is a meaningful safety credential.

Connectivity

The Second Link (Tuas) corridor access is the township's primary connectivity advantage — 5km from Singapore, significantly less congested than the Johor-Singapore Causeway. The township is served by Causeway Link's CW7L bus from Sunway Big Box, providing public transport access to Singapore for residents without cars. Internal connectivity includes pedestrian and cyclist infrastructure, and a shuttle service to Legoland.

Awards

Between 2014 and 2024, the township has received multiple recognitions including Best Smart City Award (2024), GreenRE certifications, and Best Township of the Year accolades from various Malaysian property industry bodies. These are industry awards, not government designations, but they reflect sustained investment in both quality and sustainability credentials.

What this means for buyers

Sunway City Iskandar Puteri is a genuine long-term township investment from one of Malaysia's most credible developers. The scale, the BOO model, and the integrated ecosystem distinguish it from most competing developments. The trade-off is primarily leasehold tenure on most residential units and a western corridor location that is still maturing relative to the Nusajaya core. For the right buyer — particularly families prioritising education, security, and a complete lifestyle ecosystem — it is one of the most well-rounded townships in Johor.
