Iskandar Puteri · Retirement

Retiring in Iskandar Puteri: Golf, Healthcare, and Cost of Living

Sam Tee Property Advisor · REN 80322
Iskandar Puteri, Johor

Retirement in Iskandar Puteri is not a new idea — Malaysians have been moving here from Kuala Lumpur and Penang for a decade, and Singaporeans have been considering it for just as long. What drives the decision is usually a combination of three things: space, golf, and affordable healthcare. Here is what each of those actually looks like on the ground.

Why Iskandar Puteri specifically — not just “JB”

When retirees say they are retiring to Johor Bahru, they often mean Iskandar Puteri. The distinction matters. JB Town is urban, dense, and car-dependent in a different way — it suits commuters and investors more than retirees looking for quiet and green space. Iskandar Puteri offers something different: gated landed homes, golf courses, wide roads, low density, and a pace of life that is more suburban than metropolitan.

The other relevant comparison is Forest City. Forest City offers an island lifestyle with similar greenery and golf, at lower prices — but it is more isolated, with fewer medical and commercial services nearby. Iskandar Puteri sits in the middle: greener and quieter than JB Town, but better served and more connected than Forest City.

Golf: what is available and what it costs

Iskandar Puteri has more golf courses within a 20-minute drive than almost any other residential area in Malaysia. The main options:

The Golf Club at Horizon Hills

An 18-hole championship course maintained by Gamuda Land to a consistently high standard. Residents of Horizon Hills have access to subsidised membership rates. Non-resident memberships are available. Green fees for non-members are in the range of RM 150–RM 250 on weekdays. The clubhouse has a restaurant, pro shop, and function rooms.

Legends Golf & Country Resort (Kempas)

One of Johor’s oldest and most established courses, a 20-minute drive from Iskandar Puteri. Known for its mature trees and slightly more challenging layout. Popular with older golfers who prefer course character over manicured modernity.

Senibong Golf Club

A public access course closer to Permas Jaya and JB Town, offering lower green fees and more casual play. Not as prestigious as Horizon Hills or Legends, but a practical weekday option for high-frequency golfers who want to keep costs down.

Forest City Golf Resort (30 minutes away)

Two 18-hole courses on Pulau Satu. For retirees based in Iskandar Puteri who want to experience island golf, this is a viable day-trip option. Forest City’s golf resort is world-ranked and maintained to an international standard.

For retirees who play three or four times a week, the Horizon Hills membership makes the most financial sense if you are already living in or considering buying in that township. Annual golf membership (resident pricing) has historically been structured to reward homeowners within the development.

Healthcare: private hospitals within reach

Healthcare access is the single factor that gives most retirees pause when considering any location outside Singapore or a major Malaysian city. Iskandar Puteri is better served than most assume.

Columbia Asia Hospital (列柏亚西亚医院, Nusajaya)

The closest full-service private hospital to most Iskandar Puteri townships — approximately 10–15 minutes by car from Horizon Hills or Eco Botanic. Columbia Asia is a regional chain with consistent standards across its Malaysian network. It handles general medicine, surgery, orthopaedics, and has a 24-hour emergency department. For routine specialist consultations and elective procedures, this is the go-to facility for most Iskandar Puteri residents.

KPJ Puteri Specialist Hospital (JB Town)

A larger specialist hospital approximately 25 minutes from Iskandar Puteri. KPJ is one of Malaysia’s most established private hospital groups. Strong in cardiology, oncology, and complex surgical procedures. For conditions requiring subspecialist care beyond what Columbia Asia provides, KPJ Puteri is the next referral point.

Gleneagles Medini (Iskandar Puteri)

Gleneagles is a name familiar to Singaporeans — the same group operates Gleneagles Hospital in Singapore. Gleneagles Medini is located within Medini Iskandar, in the heart of Iskandar Puteri, and is one of the most modern private hospitals in Johor. It covers cardiology, neurology, oncology, orthopaedics, and paediatrics. For Singaporeans in particular, the Gleneagles name provides a baseline of quality expectation that is recognisable and trusted.

For serious emergencies: Singapore

The honest answer is that for life-threatening emergencies, most foreign retirees and well-resourced Malaysian retirees plan to cross to Singapore. The Second Link gives Iskandar Puteri residents access to Singapore’s hospital system within approximately 30–45 minutes in normal traffic conditions. This is not a criticism of the local hospitals — it is the same logic that drives Singaporeans themselves to seek overseas treatment for certain complex conditions. Having Singapore as a backstop is one of Iskandar Puteri’s genuine advantages over retirement destinations that are further from the border.

Cost of living: the real numbers

Monthly living costs in Iskandar Puteri for a retired couple who own their home and do not commute:

  • Groceries and household supplies: RM 1,500–RM 2,500 depending on lifestyle and whether you shop at premium supermarkets (Jaya Grocer, Village Grocer) or local markets
  • Dining out: RM 800–RM 2,000 for a couple eating out regularly. A hawker meal is RM 8–RM 15 per person; a sit-down restaurant is RM 40–RM 100 per person
  • Utilities (electricity, water, internet): RM 400–RM 800 per month in a landed home with air-conditioning usage
  • Gated community maintenance fees: RM 200–RM 600 per month depending on precinct
  • Car running costs (one car, local use only): RM 500–RM 800 per month including petrol, road tax, and insurance
  • Golf (regular play): RM 500–RM 1,500 per month depending on frequency and membership status
  • Domestic helper: RM 1,800–RM 2,500 per month if applicable (very common among retirees in this area)
  • Private health insurance or regular specialist visits: RM 500–RM 2,000 per month depending on coverage and health status

A comfortable retired couple in Iskandar Puteri — owning their home outright, with one car, playing golf twice a week, eating out regularly, and maintaining good private health coverage — can live well on RM 7,000–RM 12,000 per month. At SGD/MYR 3.5, that is SGD 2,000–SGD 3,400. For Singaporeans drawing CPF Life or living on investment income in SGD, this represents a meaningful and sustainable lifestyle upgrade.

For Malaysian retirees: the KL comparison

Malaysians retiring from Kuala Lumpur or Penang to Iskandar Puteri are effectively trading urban density for suburban space at similar or lower cost. A comparable gated landed home in Mont Kiara, Damansara, or Desa ParkCity would cost more to buy, more to maintain, and provide less green space. The Iskandar Puteri alternative offers the same security and community feel, a lower landed price, and the added bonus of proximity to Singapore for occasional hospital trips, shopping, or family visits.

For Malaysian retirees who are not dependent on a Singapore income, the main consideration is whether they want to stay close to their established social network in KL or Penang, versus starting fresh in a quieter, greener setting. Many manage this by buying in Iskandar Puteri as a secondary residence first, spending school holidays or dry season months there, then making a permanent move after a year or two of testing the lifestyle.

For foreign retirees: MM2H and long-term stay

Foreign retirees who want to live in Malaysia long-term should consider the Malaysia My Second Home (MM2H) programme. MM2H grants a renewable 5-year social visit pass, allowing foreign nationals to stay in Malaysia without the restrictions of a standard tourist visa.

The requirements for MM2H as of 2026 include a minimum offshore income of RM 40,000 per month and a fixed deposit placement of RM 1 million in a Malaysian bank. These thresholds are significantly higher than earlier versions of the programme and are specifically designed to attract higher-net-worth retirees.

For Singaporeans who do not qualify for MM2H, the standard approach is a sequence of long-stay social visit passes. Malaysian immigration allows Singaporean citizens up to 90 days per visit, and many retirees manage a permanent de facto residence through a combination of short border crossings. This is a grey area legally and practically — for a clean long-term arrangement, MM2H or permanent residency (PR) through other routes is the more robust solution.

What to plan for that most people miss

  • You will need a car. Iskandar Puteri has no meaningful public transport. Grocery runs, hospital appointments, and social activities all require driving. If driving is likely to become difficult with age, plan for a domestic helper or regular transport arrangement.
  • Social life takes effort to build. Unlike an apartment building with shared amenities, a landed home in a gated township does not automatically provide social connection. Most long-term residents build their social network through the golf club, church or temple, and residents’ associations. This takes time — expect 6–12 months before a genuine community of friends is established.
  • Property management if you are away. If you split time between Iskandar Puteri and Singapore or another country, arrange reliable property management before you leave. Empty landed homes in gated communities still require maintenance, security checks, and occasional intervention for plumbing or electrical issues.
  • Test before committing. Rent for 6 to 12 months before buying. The lifestyle feels different to imagine and different to live. Renting gives you time to evaluate specific precincts, understand the school run noise and township activity patterns, and identify which precinct actually suits your daily rhythm.
