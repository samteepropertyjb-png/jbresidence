Bandar Wawari: Iskandar Puteri's Next 3,000-Acre Township

Sam Tee Property Advisor · REN 80322
Taman Wawari, Iskandar Puteri, Johor

When Bukit Indah launched in 1997, few people predicted it would take one developer 30 years to build out its 1,521 acres. Bandar Wawari is nearly double that scale — and this time, six major developers are building simultaneously. What's taking shape in Taman Wawari is the most significant new township in Iskandar Puteri in a generation.

What is Bandar Wawari?

Bandar Wawari is a planned township of approximately 3,000 acres located in Taman Wawari, Iskandar Puteri. It is a joint development — not a single-developer project — which means multiple phases, product types, and price points are being built in parallel rather than sequentially.

The developers currently active within Bandar Wawari's boundaries include:

  • IIB — Wawari Park Home (townhouses)
  • Paragon — Kensington
  • Linbaq — Ascent Park
  • KSL — Riveria (6,000+ units planned)
  • BCB — Lumina
  • Shiya — upcoming launch

Having multiple established developers build simultaneously means the township gains critical mass faster — retail, roads, and amenities do not have to wait for a single developer's cash flow cycle.

The Bukit Indah comparison — why it matters

Bukit Indah is the obvious reference point. It launched under a single developer in 1997 and has taken close to 30 years to reach the mature, established community it is today. At 1,521 acres, it is now considered one of the most liveable and liquid addresses in Iskandar Puteri.

Bandar Wawari is nearly double the land area. The multi-developer model removes the single-developer bottleneck. If even a fraction of the development pace accelerates relative to Bukit Indah, buyers who enter early are positioning ahead of a township that has not yet priced in its maturity.

Why the road access is the real story

Bandar Wawari benefits from a new road access that was recently completed — and the routing is strategically important. Every resident entering the township passes through Bandar Wawari first, regardless of whether they live there. This matters for commercial viability, but it also matters for residential demand.

The catchment served by this road includes:

  • Direct connection from JB Town
  • New access from Bukit Indah, Horizon Hills, and Nusa Idaman
  • All residents of Medini, Puteri Harbour, and UEM Estuari pass through this road before reaching their respective neighbourhoods

The surrounding population absorbing this road is estimated at 440,000 people. That is not a projection — it reflects the existing residential developments already completed or under construction in the immediate zone.

The residential pipeline: 13,000+ units

Bandar Wawari does not sit in an empty field. It sits at the centre of one of the densest residential development clusters in Iskandar Puteri. Here is the current pipeline:

Completed developments nearby

  • UEM Estuari Garden — 350 units
  • Emerald Bay — 250 units
  • Horizon Hills — 6,000 units

Under construction

  • Ascent Park — 448 units
  • IIB Townhouse — 220 units
  • KSL Riveria — 6,000+ units
  • UEM Estuari Green — 46 units
  • UEM Estuari Park Homes — 144 units
  • Suria Hills — 116 units
  • Plus further upcoming landed and high-rise launches

Total immediate residential context: over 13,000 units, the majority of which are families who will need schools, retail, F&B, and services within easy reach.

Who is moving to this corridor?

The demand base for Bandar Wawari is not speculative — it reflects the actual residents and workers already present in the surrounding area.

Surrounding residents

Families already living in Estuari, Emerald Bay, Horizon Hills, Puteri Harbour, and the wider Iskandar Puteri corridor. These are established communities with spending power and lifestyle expectations — the natural first-mover buyers for new townhouses and condominiums in Bandar Wawari.

Government and public sector workers

Pejabat Tanah, Pejabat Kerajaan Negeri Johor, and PKPJ all operate within the catchment. Government sector workers in Malaysia represent a stable and large buyer pool — typically prioritising affordability, road access, and proximity to school zones.

Court complex workers

The Johor Bahru Court Complex is located within commutable distance. Legal professionals, court support staff, and related service providers represent another consistent residential demand segment.

Education families

Two internationally recognised schools sit within the zone:

  • Marlborough College Malaysia — one of Johor's most sought-after international schools, consistently cited by expat families as a key driver of their location decision in Iskandar Puteri
  • Raffles International School — another established name pulling Singaporean and regional families into the corridor

Parent-driven property decisions around school catchment zones are among the most reliable and least price-sensitive demand segments in any residential market. Bandar Wawari sits in the right postcode for both schools.

Sam's take

Bandar Wawari is early-stage — but it is not speculative in the way that greenfield townships 20 kilometres from the nearest amenity are speculative. The surrounding residential base already exists. The road access is already built. The schools are already operating. The developers are already on site.

The question for any buyer is timing: how early is early enough to benefit from appreciation, and how early is too early that the township still feels incomplete? That answer differs depending on whether you are buying to live, to rent, or to hold.
