International Schools Near Iskandar Puteri: 2026 Guide

Sam Tee Property Advisor · REN 80322
Iskandar Puteri, Johor

International school access is one of the top three reasons families buy property in Iskandar Puteri. The schools here — British, American, Singaporean curriculum — offer a standard of education that is comparable to what families would find in Singapore, at fees that are typically 30–50% lower. Here is what is available, where it is, and how to choose a home with school proximity in mind.

Why Iskandar Puteri became an international school hub

Between 2010 and 2018, the Iskandar Regional Development Authority (IRDA) actively courted international school operators as anchor tenants for the Edu City development. The result is a cluster of globally recognised schools within a 5-kilometre radius of each other — an unusual concentration that does not exist in any other part of Malaysia outside Kuala Lumpur.

The schools in Edu City and the surrounding area hold international accreditations (CIS, WASC, IB Organisation, Cambridge Assessment) and accept students from kindergarten through to pre-university level. Many offer the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma, which is recognised by universities worldwide.

For families relocating from Singapore, this means children can continue on a broadly similar curriculum track without the disruption of switching from an internationally recognised programme to a local one.

The key international schools in and around Iskandar Puteri

Marlborough College Malaysia (Edu City)

A co-educational boarding and day school following the British curriculum, affiliated with Marlborough College in Wiltshire, England. Offers IGCSE and IB Diploma. Known for strong pastoral care and boarding facilities. A significant number of its students board from Singapore and internationally. Annual fees for day students are in the range of RM 60,000–RM 100,000 per year depending on year group.

ELC International School (Eco Botanic / Iskandar Puteri)

One of the longest-established international schools in Johor Bahru, ELC has a campus within the Iskandar Puteri corridor and is particularly popular with families from the local Chinese community and Singaporean families. Offers Cambridge IGCSE and A-Levels. Fees are lower than Marlborough, making it accessible for a wider income range.

Raffles American School (Edu City)

An American curriculum school following a US college-preparatory programme. Offers AP (Advanced Placement) courses and is accredited by AdvancED. Well regarded by families with ties to the US or who plan for university applications in the US or Canada.

Newcastle University Medicine Malaysia (NUMed)

A medical school campus affiliated with Newcastle University in the UK. Relevant to families with university-age children rather than school-age; mentioned here because its presence reinforces Edu City’s higher-education ecosystem.

Shelford International School

A newer addition to the Iskandar Puteri school landscape, offering preschool through to secondary level. Lower fees than Marlborough and Raffles, making it an entry point for families who want international schooling at a more accessible cost.

Greenview Islamic International School

An Islamic international school offering a bilingual curriculum (English and Arabic) with Islamic Studies integrated. Serves families looking for a faith-based international education environment.

Which township is closest to which school?

Geography matters. Here is how the three main townships sit relative to the schools:

  • Eco Botanic is the closest township to Edu City (Marlborough, Raffles, NUMed). The school cluster is typically a 5–10 minute drive from most Eco Botanic precincts. ELC’s Iskandar Puteri campus is also very close. This is why Eco Botanic has become the default choice for school-driven buyers.
  • Horizon Hills is slightly further from Edu City — approximately 10–15 minutes by car — but still within a comfortable school-run distance. Several Horizon Hills families send children to Marlborough and ELC.
  • East Ledang is in a similar range to Horizon Hills in terms of school drive time, though slightly more variable depending on the specific precinct and the school in question.

All three townships provide manageable school-run distances by Malaysian standards. None requires driving on major highways during peak school hours. The difference between townships is minutes, not hours.

What about Malaysian national and Chinese schools?

Iskandar Puteri also has access to established Chinese vernacular schools (SRJK(C)) and national primary schools (SK). For Malaysian families who prefer vernacular education for primary school — a common choice even among higher-income Malaysians — schools such as SRJK(C) Chong Hwa and several national schools serve the corridor.

The typical approach among Malaysian families in Iskandar Puteri is vernacular or national primary school, then transition to international school at secondary level when subject choices and curriculum tracks become more significant. Property near a good SRJK(C) and within reach of an international secondary school covers both phases.

Fees: what to budget

International school fees in Iskandar Puteri are meaningfully lower than comparable schools in Singapore. As a rough benchmark for 2026:

  • Marlborough College Malaysia: RM 70,000–RM 100,000 per year (day student)
  • Raffles American School: RM 55,000–RM 80,000 per year
  • ELC International School: RM 25,000–RM 45,000 per year
  • Shelford International School: RM 20,000–RM 35,000 per year

For Singaporean families: at SGD/MYR 3.5, even Marlborough’s top tier at RM 100,000 translates to approximately SGD 28,600 per year — a fraction of what comparable British curriculum schools charge in Singapore.

How to factor schools into your property search

Several practical points are worth building into your property shortlist:

  • Visit before you commit: School proximity looks similar on a map but varies significantly in practice. A 5-minute drive on a weekend morning may be 20 minutes at 7:45am on a school day. Do the school run in real time before deciding.
  • Check admission status: Popular schools — especially Marlborough and Raffles — have waiting lists. Proximity to the school does not guarantee a place. Confirm your child’s admission status before using school proximity as a deciding factor in your property purchase.
  • Plan for age changes: If you have young children, consider which schools serve which age groups. You may want primary near one campus and secondary near another. Eco Botanic’s central position is partly valuable because it sits equidistant from multiple options.
  • For foreigners on MM2H or work passes: International school access is generally unrestricted for children of foreign residents. Some schools prioritise admission for children of permanent residents or work pass holders over tourist visa holders, so documentation matters.

The broader picture

Iskandar Puteri’s international school cluster is one of its most durable advantages. Schools do not move. The investment in Edu City infrastructure — which includes the schools, university campuses, and purpose-built educational facilities — represents a long-term anchor for the area’s property demand. Families drive the most stable segment of residential rental and owner-occupier demand, and those families are anchored here by their children’s schools.

For buyers making a 10–15 year commitment to the area — the natural time horizon of a family move tied to children’s school years — Iskandar Puteri’s school ecosystem is a genuine structural advantage.
